PlanMyRoute · Stage 1 · Visual Foundations

Three directions for the open road.

Each is a complete foundation — palette, type, and the base components that carry dense itinerary, map, and expense data. They are deliberately different in temperature and energy so the choice is real. Every direction is built light-first, because a phone screen has to survive direct sunlight at a fuel stop. Pick one, or mix — then we apply it to screens in Stage 2.

01 — Golden Hour 02 — Wayfinding 03 — Horizon
DIRECTION 01

Golden Hour

Sun-baked warmth and asphalt ink. The temperature of late-afternoon light hitting a dusty windshield — optimistic, analog, made for being read outdoors.

Best sunlight legibility
Palette & reasoning
Sand Cream
#F7F1E5
The base. A warm paper-white scatters glare instead of blasting it back at you — far easier on the eyes in sun than a clinical pure white.
Asphalt Ink
#1B1916
Text and structure. A warm near-black, not flat #000 — ~15:1 contrast on cream so dates, totals and labels stay sharp in bright light.
Sunset Coral
#F1512A
The one true accent. Departure energy — primary actions, the active route line, "go". Used sparingly so it always means something.
Dust Amber
#F0A93A
Warm secondary — highlights, premium touches, refuel stops. Pairs with coral for a two-tone sunrise without going neon.
Olive Trail
#7C7A52
Calm support for maps, nature stops, "viewer" states. Desaturated so it never fights the warm accents.
Canyon Clay
#B5654A
Earthy depth for accommodation stops and secondary tags. Keeps the family grounded and analog.

DIRECTION 02

Wayfinding

Borrowed from national-park signage and topographic maps. Sturdy, structured, instantly legible — the app as a trustworthy guide that brings order to the chaos.

Most structured / map-native
Palette & reasoning
Topo Paper
#F2F0E8
Map-paper off-white. Reads as a printed trail map and stays calm under a lot of pins and labels.
Spruce Ink
#10241D
Deep forest near-black. Authoritative like signage type; ~14:1 on paper for full-sun legibility.
Highway Orange
#FF6A1A
Construction-sign orange = "you are here / act now". Maximum visibility, even through a dusty screen.
Route Blue
#1F62D6
Interstate-shield blue for routes, links and the map line. A familiar wayfinding cue that means "the path".
Trail Tan
#C9BA97
Contour-line tan for dividers, map fills and quiet surfaces. The texture of paper maps.
Pine Sign
#0E7C57
Park-sign green for nature stops, "approved/paid" states and success. Completes the signage system.

DIRECTION 03

Horizon

The electric moment the sun drops and the night begins. The boldest, most social option — twilight gradients used as rare, expressive punctuation over crisp, high-contrast surfaces.

Boldest / most youthful
Palette & reasoning
Haze White
#F4F2F7
A faintly cool off-white base. Keeps the vivid accents feeling electric while staying glare-friendly outdoors.
Midnight Ink
#16131F
Near-black with a violet undertone. ~16:1 on haze — all data and body text sit here, never on a gradient.
Electric Indigo
Primary. Night-drive energy and the social/AI layer. Solid (not gradient) on buttons so contrast stays measurable.
#6A4DF4
Tangerine
#FF7A2F
The warm counterweight — the sun in the gradient. Routes, "go" actions, energy moments.
Hot Pink
#FF4D8D
Social pop — likes, follows, community events. The third gradient stop; used only in the social layer.
Dusk Gradient
indigo → tangerine
Reserved for hero/empty/premium moments only — never behind text or dense data.

How to choose

These are deliberately spread across a spectrum: Golden Hour is the warmest and safest outdoors, Wayfinding is the most structured and map-native, Horizon is the boldest and most social. Mixing is encouraged — e.g. Wayfinding's stop-type color coding with Horizon's type, or Golden Hour's palette with Wayfinding's signage display.
